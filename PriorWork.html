Embedded systems: Autonomous mars rover

For the final porject of CPRE 288 me and a three other students had to write a program that would run on an ATmega16 board. This board controlled a roomba robot that had infrared and sonar sensors attached to it. The goal of the program was use the sensors to autonomously guide the robot through a maze of pylons, small rocks, and holes to a "landing zone" identifiable by a pattern in the pylons and a colored circle on the floor. I led the team which involved three electrical engineers from both Asia and Africa. As the main programmer I was in charge of distributing the workload (taking the bulk of it myself) and helping the others understand the C language. I learned how to communicate with others and how to read the technical specifications about the different components and about the robot. I also learned some of the finer points of memory management.
