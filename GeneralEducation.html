How my general education helps me as a software engineer

My general electives have been focused on mainly psychology and economics. Psychology helps me understand people so that I can clearly communicate with them and understand what it is they are trying to communicate. Since people come from many different backgrounds and thought processes the more I understand them the better we'll be able to work together. Economics allows my to always have the big picture in mind as I consider the detials of how my work will effect the company and the market. economics is closely tied with business of all kinds so it is only natural that I learn it and learn it well to be successful in my future workplace.

Specific instances where I used my general education in engineering

In my first internship I was still learning the finer points of coding. I realized that since I was paid about half of what my mentor made it would only be worth it to ask him for help if he could explain a concept in 1 hour that would take me over 3 to grasp. The reason for this is because 1 hour of him explaining and me listening would cost the company the same if I took 3 hours on my own to understand the concept. By understanding this I was able to be effient with my time and give the company the most value per paycheck I could.
